Theme 1: The Psychology of Reward Culture

Why explicitly structured rewards engineer elite technical outputs

"If you build it, they might look. If you build it, transparently score it, and reward it on a brightly lit national stage, teams will push optimization boundaries past what you thought possible. High-performance AI isn't built out of thin air; it's fueled by clear visibility and stable oxygen."

Throughout my trajectory as an undergraduate in Malaysia, a Ph.D. candidate in Australia, and an international competition chair, I have observed that the difference between an exceptional technical deployment and a project that quietly stalls boils down to the Reward Framework. Deep baseline execution requires four pillars:

01

Clear Goal

Explicitly defined clinical boundaries. Teams optimize models for actual operational parameters, not generic performance arrays.

02

Fair Rules

Stabilised, non-shifting rubrics published on Day 1. Complete transparency protects structural trust throughout execution.

03

Real Reward

Tangible milestones—funding, institutional credentials, public podium space, and direct access to systemic decision-makers.

04

Public Recognition

National broadcast, executive features, and community impact profiles. Total public transparency drives absolute project commitment.

Theme 2: Quick-Win Projects

The Agile Deployment Engine: Small, Fast, Visible, Useful

Grand strategies look beautiful in slide decks, but they risk dying in protracted steering committee meetings. True structural transformation relies on shipping functional, clinical code within compact windows.

1. Radically Small Scope

Zero in on an explicit workflow friction point. Focus on an isolated ward queue, a specific predictive diagnostic channel, or a single bottlenecked administrative protocol rather than trying to fix the whole institution at once.

2. Rapid Delivery (2–4 Weeks)

Avoid lengthy 18-month architectural roadmaps. Build, validate, and ship an operational solution within a month to maintain momentum and team focus.

3. High Tangibility & Visibility

Ensure that frontline Medical Officers, nurses, and administrative specialists can directly interact with the tool on their screens by Monday morning.

4. Clear, Immediate Utility

Focus on concrete, functional value—like saving a department exactly 12 minutes per shift—rather than abstract, long-term conceptual metrics.

5. Concise One-Page Documentation

Create a simple, accessible summary of performance metrics, data safety compliance, and workflow impacts that institutional directors can easily present up the chain of command.

The Evaluation Rubric Framework

Instead of judging on raw model accuracy alone, we published a balanced evaluation matrix on Day 1. This framework explicitly prioritized safe, transparent clinical reasoning over opaque black-box optimizations:

  • Mathematical & Algorithmic Rigor: 25%
  • Model Performance & Validation: 20%
  • Problem Definition & Clinical Relevance: 15%
  • Innovation & Creativity: 15%
  • Clinical Interpretability & Safety: 10%
  • "Hope" Impact & Practical Deployment: 10%
  • Presentation Quality & Clarity: 5%

Innovathon Playbook

Six actionable lessons to take away from this talk

If you take away practical frameworks from this plenary session to deploy within your hospital groups, let it be these six core rules:

Reward Publicly and Immediately

Pair transparent funding allocations with direct institutional pathways and senior recognition. Make breakthroughs feel significant to the entire organization.

Publish Stable Rubrics Early

Define success explicitly before any development begins. Keep these guidelines completely consistent to maintain trust and clear targets.

Mix Evaluation Domain Experts

Combine frontline clinical staff, data scientists, policy developers, and operations managers. Balanced perspectives ensure solutions work well in daily practice.

Provide Open, Structured Mentorship

Run open workshops during the building process. Ongoing technical guidance eliminates dead ends early and keeps project standards high.

Focus on Workflow Stories, Not Raw Scores

Look beyond abstract accuracy percentages. Value tools for concrete operational outcomes, like reducing a specific task's turnaround time down to 52 seconds.

Celebrate Milestones Like Major Institutional Steps

Turn final project wrap-ups into key professional events. Genuine high-visibility recognition keeps teams motivated and eager to return for future challenges.
